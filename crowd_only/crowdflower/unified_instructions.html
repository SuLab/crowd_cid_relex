Title: Determine whether a chemical contributes to a disease

Overview

Doctors need to know what diseases a chemical or drug may cause when creating perscriptions. In this task, you will determine whether a passage of text says a chemical contributes to a disease.

The Task

  1. All mentions of one chemical and one disease have been identified and highlighted in a passage of text. Sentences containing both the chemical and disease are underlined.
  2. Chemicals or diseases may have multiple names (e.g., rapamycin and sirolimus). Assume that anything highlighted with the same colour is the same thing.
  3. Use the entire text to determine whether the text says that the chemical is contributing to or causing the disease (for example, as a side effect). Select between:
    • [chemical] directly contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the text says that the chemical (by itself or with other chemicals) directly contributes to or causes the highlighted disease.
    • The text does not say that [chemical] contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the text does not say that the chemical contributes to the disease.

Examples

  1. The chemotherapy drug feverolinus has multiple side effects, including diarrhea, constipation, and hair loss.
    Correct answer: Feverolinus directly contributes to or causes hair loss.
  2. The government has deemed Lipitor safe for use in patients with high cholesterol.
    Correct answer: The text does not say that Lipitor contributes to or causes high cholesterol.
  3. BACKGROUND: To determine the efficacy of Quentin in a clinical setting.
    …
    EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN: A total of 1000 patients were recruited. Patients were given Quentin and monitored for their response over 4 weeks.
    …
    RESULTS: Most (65%) patients had no problems. Grade >= 3 peripheral hematoma and whooping cough arose in 3% of patients.
    A passage will often first discuss what was tested in the background or introduction. Read the results section to determine what the scientists found.
    Correct answer: Quentin contributes to or causes peripheral hematoma.
  4. Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) is a type of cancer that affects white blood cells, and progression of CML can be slowed with corticosteroids. Capecitabine, a corticosteroid, is effective for treating CML in young adults. Side effects, though rare, may include gastrointestinal hemorrhage.
    Do not confuse groups of chemicals (corticosteroids) with specific chemicals (capecitabine). The side effect mentioned here (gastrointestinal hemorrhage) is caused by capecitabine, not by corticosteroids.
    Correct answer: The text does not say that corticosteroids contributes to or causes gastrointestinal hemorrhage.
  5. A cohort of 100 men was given tanamycin and maximidin and monitored for increases in heart rate. The dose of convamin was 10 mg/kg. Four hours later, 15% of the men complained of dizziness.
    Although not explicit, the text heavily implies that the two drugs maximidin and tanamycin/convamin worked together to cause the dizziness.
    Correct answer: tanamycin/convamin directly contributes to or causes dizziness.
  6. A national mental health study found that 60% of patients developed joint pain in association with dinotefuran beta-hydroxylase antagonists.
    Protein names often include a chemical that the protein interacts with. Groups of chemicals are often named based on which proteins they target. Do not confuse the specific chemical in the name with the protein or group of chemicals. Although dinotefuran is a chemical, in context, the cause of joint pain is "dinotefuran beta-hydroxylase antagonists", which is a group of chemicals which bind to the protein "dinotefuran beta-hydroxylase".
    Correct answer: The text does not say that dinotefuran contributes to or causes joint pain.
