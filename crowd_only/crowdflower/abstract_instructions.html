Title: Determine whether a chemical contributes to a disease

Overview

Scientists and doctors need to know whether a chemical (such as Aspirin) can potentially cause any diseases before they can give it to people. In this task, you will determine whether a passage of text says a chemical contributes to a disease.

The Task

  1. One chemical and one disease have been identified and highlighted in a passage of text. Sometimes a drug or disease may have multiple names (e.g., rapamycin and sirolimus). Assume that anything highlighted with the same colour is the same thing.
  2. Read the text and determine whether the text says that the chemical is contributing to or causing the disease (for example, as a side effect). Choose one of the three possible choices:
    • [chemical] directly contributes to or causes [disease]
      Select this choice when the text says that the chemical (by itself or with other chemicals) directly contributes to or causes the highlighted disease.
    • [chemical] contributes to another disease, and that other disease causes [disease]
      Select this choice when the chemical does not cause the disease directly, but rather the chemical causes another disease, and that other disease causes the highlighted disease.
    • The text does not say that [chemical] contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the text does not say that the chemical contributes to the disease.
  3. Rate how confident you are in your answer. Any choice is ok.

Examples

  1. A cohort of 100 men was given tanamycin and monitored for increases in heart rate. The dose of convamin was 10 mg/kg. Four hours later, 15% of the men complained of dizziness.
    Here the chemical has two names: tanamycin and convamin. They both mean the same thing. When there are multiple names, all the names will be given in the answer choice. The correct choice is:
    tanamycin/convamin directly contributes to or causes dizziness
  2. Heart attacks are a well known downstream effect of barbatimol-induced artherosclerosis.
    Artherosclerosis (plaque buildup in arteries) causes heart attacks by restricting blood flow. Since barbatimol is inducing (causing) artherosclerosis, it is not directly contributing to heart attacks. Therefore the correct choice is:
    barbatimol contributes to another disease, and that other disease causes heart attacks
  3. Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the USA. Together with cancer, these two diseases claim over a million American lives each year. Our clinical results indicate that Flibitor can drastically lower the chances of developing heart disease in patients over 50.
    Here cancer is mentioned as another leading cause of death. It has nothing to do with Flibitor and therefore the correct choice is:
    The text does not say that Flibitor contributes to or causes cancer
