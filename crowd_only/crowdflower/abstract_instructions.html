Title: Determine whether a chemical contributes to a disease

Overview

Doctors need to know whether a chemical (such as Aspirin) can potentially cause any diseases before they can give it to people. In this task, you will determine whether a passage of text says a chemical contributes to a disease.

The Task

  1. One chemical and one disease have been identified and highlighted in a passage of text. A chemical or disease may have multiple names (e.g., rapamycin and sirolimus). Assume that anything highlighted with the same colour is the same thing. All names will be listed in the answer choice.
  2. Read the text and determine whether the text says that the chemical is contributing to or causing the disease (for example, as a side effect). Choose one of the two possible choices:
    • [chemical] directly contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the text says that the chemical (by itself or with other chemicals) directly contributes to or causes the highlighted disease.
    • The text does not say that [chemical] contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the text does not say that the chemical contributes to the disease.

Examples

  1. Feverolinus, a chemotherapy drug derived from mushrooms and used for treating breast cancer, has multiple side effects, including diarrhea, constipation, and hair loss.
    Feverolinus is a chemotherapy drug that has multiple side effects, including hair loss. The correct choice is:
    Feverolinus directly contributes to or causes hair loss.
  2. Some of the patients, especially those over 50 years old, complained of experiencing chest aches and lower back pain two hours after taking adalamin and maximidin.
    Maximidin is working together with the chemical adalamin to cause chest aches. The correct choice is:
    maximidin directly contributes to or causes chest aches.
  3. A cohort of 100 men was given tanamycin and monitored for increases in heart rate. The dose of convamin was 10 mg/kg. Four hours later, 15% of the men complained of dizziness.
    The chemical has two equivalent names: tanamycin and convamin. They both mean the same thing. The correct choice is:
    tanamycin/convamin directly contributes to or causes dizziness.
  4. Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the USA. Together with cancer, these two diseases claim over a million American lives each year. Our clinical results indicate that Flibitor can drastically lower the chances of developing heart disease in patients over 50.
    Here cancer is mentioned as another leading cause of death. It has nothing to do with Flibitor and therefore the correct choice is:
    The text does not say that Flibitor contributes to or causes cancer.
  5. The Food and Drug Administration has concluded its review of Pfizer's Lipitor and deemed it safe and efficacious for lowering high cholesterol.
    The sentence says that Lipitor lowers high cholesterol, which means it is curing the disease. The correct choice is:
    The sentence does not say that Lipitor contributes to or causes high cholesterol.
  6. To evaluate the efficacy and safety of levofloxacin (500 mg orally once daily for 10 to 14 days) in treating adult outpatients with acute bacterial sinusitis.
    Levofloxacin is being evaluated as a treatment for sinusitis. The correct choice is:
    The sentence does not say that levofloxacin contributes to or causes sinusitis.
  7. During the infusion of aminophylline, the ventricular fibrillation threshold was reduced by 30 to 40% of the control when pH and partial pressures of oxygen (PO2) and carbon dioxide (CO2) were kept within normal limits.
    In this sentence, PO2 means "partial pressure of oxygen", which is not a chemical. The highlighting made a mistake. The correct choice is:
    The text does not say that PO2 contributes to or causes ventricular fibrillation.
