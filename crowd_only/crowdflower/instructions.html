Title: Finding chemicals which contribute to disease

Overview

Scientists and doctors need to know whether a chemical (such as Aspirin) can potentially cause any diseases before they can give the chemical as a drug to patients.

In this task, you will be helping scientists identify which chemicals contribute to which diseases from a passage of text.

The Task

  1. A small number of chemicals and diseases have been identified in a passage of text. All occurrences of the same chemical and disease have been highlighted, and replaced with identifiers like [chemical A] and [disease P] to make reading easier.
  2. Some possible combinations of chemicals contributing to or causing diseases are given. A chemical "contributes to or plays a role in" a disease if and only if the chemical, by itself or in combination with other chemicals, can directly bring about the disease. Some choices will be empty ("Do not choose this choice."), and should not be selected.
  3. Read through the provided text and determine all of the choices explicitly supported by the text. If none of the choices are explicitly stated by the text, then choose "None of the provided choices are correct." by itself.
  4. Please also rate how difficult you thought it was to read the passage of text.

Examples

  1. "[disease R] is a rare but potentially serious complication of high-dose [chemical B] therapy administered prior to taking an X-ray."
    • The only correct choice is: "[chemical B] contributes to or plays a role in [disease R]."
  2. "Ten patients with [disease P] ([disease P]) treated with [chemical A] experienced exacerbation or de novo appearance of [disease Q]."
    • Long chemical or disease names are usually given an acronym, and are included in parentheses immediately after. For example, "chronic pulmonary heart disease (CPHD)". Here [disease P] followed this format and was therefore highlighted twice.
    • "De novo appearance" means "new appearance". The patients already had [disease P] before being given [chemical A], and therefore the only correct answer is: "[chemical A] contributes to or plays a role in [disease Q]".
  3. "Each year, thousands of Americans over 50 develop [disease T]. [disease T] is a rare uncontrolled contraction of the fingers that occurs in high-stress individuals. The exact cause is unknown. In the past five years, various drug candidates for [disease S] ([chemical A], [chemical B], and [chemical C]) failed to meet FDA safety regulations due to a negative interaction with [disease T]."
    • The only correct answers are:
      1. [chemical A] contributes to or plays a role in [disease T]
      2. [chemical B] contributes to or plays a role in [disease T]
      3. [chemical C] contributes to or plays a role in [disease T]
  4. "Data from the recent clinical study indicate that the combination therapy of [chemical C] and [chemical E] causes [disease Q] and induces [disease R] in 35% of patients."
    • There are only 4 correct answers:
      1. [chemical C] contributes to or plays a role in [disease Q]
      2. [chemical C] contributes to or plays a role in [disease R]
      3. [chemical E] contributes to or plays a role in [disease Q]
      4. [chemical E] contributes to or plays a role in [disease R]
