Title: Determine whether a sentence says a chemical contributes to a disease

Overview

Doctors need to know whether a chemical (such as Aspirin) causes any side effects before they can give it to people. In this task, you will determine whether a sentence says a chemical contributes to or causes a disease.

The Task

  1. One chemical and one disease have been identified and highlighted in a sentence. A chemical or disease may have multiple names (e.g., rapamycin and sirolimus). Assume that anything highlighted with the same colour is the same thing. All names will be listed in the answer choice.
  2. Read the sentence and determine whether it says that the chemical is contributing to or causing the disease (for example, as a side effect). Choose one of the three possible choices:
    • [chemical] directly contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the sentence says that the chemical (by itself or together with other chemicals) directly contributes to or causes the highlighted disease.
    • [chemical]-induced [other disease] causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the highlighted chemical causes another disease, and the other disease causes the highlighted disease. Choose this when the sentence uses a format like: [chemical]-induced [other disease] caused [disease].
    • The sentence does not say that [chemical] contributes to or causes [disease].
      Select this choice when the sentence does not say that the chemical contributes to the disease.

Examples

  1. Feverolinus, a chemotherapy drug derived from mushrooms and used for treating breast cancer, has multiple side effects, including diarrhea, constipation, and hair loss.
    Feverolinus is a chemotherapy drug that has multiple side effects, including hair loss. The correct choice is:
    Feverolinus directly contributes to or causes hair loss.
  2. Recent studies found that adalamin and maximidin, only when taken together, cause chest aches, but do not cause chest aches when taken separately.
    When a chemical only causes the disease when taken in combination with other chemicals, the correct choice is still that the chemical causes the disease. Here maximidin is working together with adalamin to cause chest aches. The correct choice is:
    maximidin directly contributes to or causes chest aches.
  3. Heart attacks are a well known downstream effect of barbatimol-induced artherosclerosis.
    The sentence says that heart attacks were caused by artherosclerosis (plaque buildup in arteries). Since this sentence follows the [chemical]-induced [other disease] causes [disease] structure, the correct choice is:
    barbatimol-induced [other disease] causes heart attacks.
  4. The Food and Drug Administration has concluded its review of Pfizer's Lipitor and deemed it safe and efficacious for lowering high cholesterol.
    The sentence says that Lipitor lowers high cholesterol, which means it is curing the disease. The correct choice is:
    The sentence does not say that Lipitor contributes to or causes high cholesterol.
  5. During the infusion of aminophylline, the ventricular fibrillation threshold was reduced by 30 to 40% of the control when pH and partial pressures of oxygen (PO2) and carbon dioxide (CO2) were kept within normal limits.
    In this sentence, PO2 means "partial pressure of oxygen", which is not a chemical. The highlighting made a mistake. The correct choice is:
    The sentence does not say that PO2 contributes to or causes ventricular fibrillation.
