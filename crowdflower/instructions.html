Title: Finding diseases caused by drugs

Overview

While most drugs are generally beneficial, some drugs unfortunately can cause side effects. When this happens, it is said that the drug causes, or induces the disease.

In this task, you will be helping scientists find drug induced diseases from a piece of text.

The Task

  1. Read a section of text.
    A small number of drugs and diseases have been highlighted in the text. All occurrences of the same drug or disease have been highlighted.
  2. Read through the list of potential drug induced diseases and pick those which are supported by the text.

Examples

    • "Rombin cystitis is a rare but potentially serious complication of high-dose wertaximab therapy administered prior to taking an X-ray."
    • In the above sentence, the drug wertaximab induces Rombin cystitis because Rombin cystitis is a "complication" of receiving the drug.
    • "Ten patients with hyperstosis (HS) treated with ramotrigine experienced exacerbation or de novo appearance of myotonic jerks."
    • "De novo appearance" means "new appearance". Although there are two different diseases in the sentence above, the only drug induced disease relationship is "ramotrigine induces myotonic jerks".
    • "Each year, thousands of Americans over 50 develop tension-associated spasmidosis (TAS). TAS is a rare uncontrolled contraction of the fingers that occurs in high-stress individuals. The exact cause is unknown. In the past five years, various drug candidates for high blood pressure (tetraforzine, 6-inositol, and 2,2-benzaime) failed to meet FDA safety regulations due to a negative interaction with TAS."
    • There are 3 drug-induced disease relationships here:
      1. tetraforzine incudes tension-associated spasmidosis
      2. 6-inositol induces tension-associated spasmidosis
      3. 2,2-benzaime induces tension-associated spasmidosis
