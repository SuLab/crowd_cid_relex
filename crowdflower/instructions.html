Title: Finding drugs which contribute to disease

Overview

While most drugs are beneficial, some drugs unfortunately can cause or contribute to unwanted side effects or diseases. This is obviously not desirable behaviour---scientists and doctors are greatly interested in whether a drug can cause side effects because this helps them decide how to treat patients.

In this task, you will be helping scientists identify which drugs contribute to diseases in a piece of text.

The Task

  1. Read a section of text.
    A computer has highlighted a small number of drugs and diseases in the text. All occurrences of the same drug or disease have been highlighted.
  2. Read through the given list of potential drug-disease interactions and select those which are explicitly supported by the text you just read. Do not select the choices which say "This choice intentionally left blank."

Examples

    • "Rombin cystitis is a rare but potentially serious complication of high-dose wertaximab therapy administered prior to taking an X-ray."
    • The only correct choice is: "wertaximab contributes to or is a marker for Rombin cystitis."
    • "Ten patients with hyperstosis (HS) treated with ramotrigine experienced exacerbation or de novo appearance of myotonic jerks."
    • "De novo appearance" means "new appearance". Although there are two different diseases in the sentence above, the only correct answer is: "ramotrigine contributes to or is a marker for myotonic jerks".
    • "Each year, thousands of Americans over 50 develop tension-associated spasmidosis (TAS). TAS is a rare uncontrolled contraction of the fingers that occurs in high-stress individuals. The exact cause is unknown. In the past five years, various drug candidates for high blood pressure (tetraforzine, 6-inositol, and 2,2-benzaime) failed to meet FDA safety regulations due to a negative interaction with TAS."
    • The only correct answers are:
      1. tetraforzine contributes to or is a marker for tension-associated spasmidosis
      2. 6-inositol contributes to or is a marker for tension-associated spasmidosis
      3. 2,2-benzaime contributes to or is a marker for tension-associated spasmidosis
    • "Data from the recent clinical study indicate that the combination therapy of 3-toribazine and Quertimab causes nausea and induces muscle spasms in 35% of patients."
    • There are only 4 correct answers:
      1. 3-toribazine contributes to or is a marker for nausea
      2. 3-toribazine contributes to or is a marker for muscle spasms
      3. Quertimab contributes to or is a marker for nausea
      4. Quertimab contributes to or is a marker for muscle spasms
