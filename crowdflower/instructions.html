Title: Finding drugs which contribute to disease

Overview

Scientists and doctors need to know whether a drug (such as Aspirin) can potentially cause any diseases before they can give the drug to patients.

In this task, you will be helping scientists identify which drugs contribute to which diseases from a piece of text.

The Task

  1. A small number of drugs and diseases have been highlighted in some text. All occurrences of the same drug or disease have been highlighted.
  2. It is possible that the drugs contribute to the diseases, and some choices (at most 5) are given. If a drug by itself, or in combination with other drugs, can bring about the disease, then it is considered to "contribute to or play a role in" the disease. Some choices will be empty ("Do not choose this choice."), and should not be selected.
  3. Read through the provided text and determine all of the choices explicitly supported by the text. If none of the choices are explicitly stated by the text, then choose "None of the provided choices are correct." by itself.

Examples

  1. "Rombin cystitis is a rare but potentially serious complication of high-dose wertaximab therapy administered prior to taking an X-ray."
    • The only correct choice is: "wertaximab contributes to or plays a role in Rombin cystitis."
  2. "Ten patients with hyperstosis (HS) treated with ramotrigine experienced exacerbation or de novo appearance of myotonic jerks."
    • "De novo appearance" means "new appearance". Although there are two different diseases in the sentence above, the only correct answer is: "ramotrigine contributes to or plays a role in myotonic jerks".
  3. "Each year, thousands of Americans over 50 develop tension-associated spasmidosis (TAS). TAS is a rare uncontrolled contraction of the fingers that occurs in high-stress individuals. The exact cause is unknown. In the past five years, various drug candidates for high blood pressure (tetraforzine, 6-inositol, and 2,2-benzaime) failed to meet FDA safety regulations due to a negative interaction with TAS."
    • The only correct answers are:
      1. tetraforzine contributes to or plays a role in tension-associated spasmidosis
      2. 6-inositol contributes to or plays a role in tension-associated spasmidosis
      3. 2,2-benzaime contributes to or plays a role in tension-associated spasmidosis
  4. "Data from the recent clinical study indicate that the combination therapy of 3-toribazine and Quertimab causes nausea and induces muscle spasms in 35% of patients."
    • There are only 4 correct answers:
      1. 3-toribazine contributes to or plays a role in nausea
      2. 3-toribazine contributes to or plays a role in muscle spasms
      3. Quertimab contributes to or plays a role in nausea
      4. Quertimab contributes to or plays a role in muscle spasms
